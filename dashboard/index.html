Business Insights Generated

Data-backed findings from behavioral analysis, cohort segmentation, and statistical experimentation — each directly actionable for improving retention and conversion outcomes.

26.6% Funnel Drop-off at Declaration

Over a quarter of students abandon at the first recovery step. Reducing this bottleneck by 50% would increase final approvals by an estimated 15.9%.

Low Study Time Predicts Quiz Failure

Students spending less than 3 minutes on study material had near-zero quiz pass probability. Study duration is the strongest behavioral predictor of recovery success.

Push Notifications Improved Engagement +17.8%

A/B testing confirmed that push notifications significantly increased session frequency. Result validated via Student's t-test with statistical significance (p < 0.001).

Professor Approval Variance Creates Bottleneck

Problem Statement

Analytical Challenges Addressed

In most institutions, attendance recovery is a manual, opaque process. There is no visibility into behavioral patterns, no early warning system, and no data-driven framework for measuring intervention effectiveness.

📉

No Funnel Visibility

7-stage recovery process with no tracking of stage-by-stage conversion, drop-off rates, or bottleneck identification.

🔍

No Behavioral Segmentation

All students treated uniformly despite dramatically different engagement patterns, risk levels, and recovery likelihood.

📊

No Statistical Validation

Intervention decisions made without hypothesis testing, controlled experimentation, or significance analysis.

⚠️

No Churn Early Warning

872 students at high churn risk (>70%) with no predictive system to identify and prioritize proactive intervention.

System Design

End-to-End Analytics Architecture

An 8-layer analytics pipeline spanning event-level behavioral tracking, dimensional data modeling, statistical analysis, predictive modeling, and intervention intelligence.

1

Event-Level Data Generation

Simulated 592K behavioral events using statistical distributions — modeling app opens, absences, study sessions, quiz attempts, and professor decisions.

2

Data Warehouse

Structured storage with dimensional modeling — fact tables for events/sessions, dimension tables for users/professors, indexed for sub-second analytical querying.

3

ETL Pipeline

Extract-Transform-Load pipeline converting raw event logs into user profiles, session aggregates, daily KPI metrics, cohort assignments, and experiment groups.

4

Analytics Engine

Compute conversion funnels, cohort retention curves (D1/D7/D30), DAU/WAU stickiness ratios, and a weighted composite Product Health Score.

5

Predictive Analytics

K-Means behavioral segmentation (4 clusters), Random Forest churn prediction (AUC 0.96), and Isolation Forest anomaly detection across daily metrics.

6

Insight Generation

Automated extraction of data-driven findings ranked by business impact — behavioral correlations, operational bottlenecks, and engagement patterns.

7

Statistical Experimentation

Controlled A/B testing with Student's t-test, confidence interval analysis, and intervention impact measurement using behavioral cohort comparisons.

8

Interactive Analytics Dashboard

9-section analytical interface with segment-filtered KPIs, drill-down funnels, retention heatmaps, and scenario simulation capabilities.
