About This Project

The Story Behind AttendIQ

AttendIQ was built to answer a fundamental question: Can we use behavioral data science to transform a broken, manual attendance recovery process into an intelligent, data-driven system? Here's the full picture.

🤔

Why This Exists

In most educational institutions, attendance recovery is a black box — students submit make-up work, professors approve or reject it, and no one tracks what happens in between. There is zero visibility into where students drop off, which interventions work, or who is at risk of giving up entirely.

AttendIQ was built to bring product analytics thinking (the kind used by Netflix, Spotify, and Duolingo) to this overlooked educational process. The goal: treat each student's recovery journey like a user funnel, measure every stage, predict churn before it happens, and statistically validate which interventions actually move the needle.

💡 This is not just a dashboard — it's a Decision Science framework that generates actionable, evidence-based recommendations.
📦

Data Source & Methodology

The dataset was synthetically generated using statistical distributions that mirror real-world educational behavioral patterns. We modeled 5,000 unique students across a 90-day semester, generating 592,000+ event-level records.

Each user's journey includes: app opens, absence declarations, PDF study sessions (with realistic duration distributions), quiz attempts and scores, and professor approval decisions. The data captures real behavioral variance — Power Users who complete 70+ sessions, At-Risk students who barely engage, and Churned users who abandon the process entirely.

📊 All correlations, retention curves, and segment behaviors emerge naturally from the simulated data — nothing is hardcoded.
🏗️

How We Built It

AttendIQ follows an 8-layer end-to-end pipeline, from raw event generation to interactive decision support:

  1. Data Generation — Python script simulates 592K events using Poisson, Normal, and Beta distributions calibrated from educational research.
  2. Data Warehouse — SQLite database with star-schema design: fact tables (events, sessions) and dimension tables (users, professors).
  3. ETL Pipeline — Pandas-powered extraction transforms raw logs into user profiles, session aggregates, and daily KPI time-series.
  4. Analytics Engine — Computes conversion funnels, cohort retention (D1/D7/D30), stickiness ratios, and a weighted Product Health Score.
  5. ML Models — K-Means clustering (4 segments), Random Forest churn predictor (AUC 0.9655), Isolation Forest anomaly detection.
  6. A/B Testing — Student's two-sample t-test validates 3 intervention experiments with 95% confidence intervals.
  7. Insight Engine — Automated discovery of patterns, correlations, and operational bottlenecks ranked by business impact.
  8. Interactive Dashboard — 18 Chart.js visualizations with cross-filtering, drill-downs, and a data-driven scenario simulator.
⚡ Zero external dependencies — the entire frontend runs on vanilla JavaScript. No React, no npm, no build step.

Students spending less than 3 minutes on study material had near-zero quiz pass probability. Study duration is the strongest behavioral predictor of recovery success.

Push Notifications Improved Engagement +17.8%

Problem Statement

Analytical Challenges Addressed

In most institutions, attendance recovery is a manual, opaque process. There is no visibility into behavioral patterns, no early warning system, and no data-driven framework for measuring intervention effectiveness.

📉

No Funnel Visibility

7-stage recovery process with no tracking of stage-by-stage conversion, drop-off rates, or bottleneck identification.

🔍

No Behavioral Segmentation

All students treated uniformly despite dramatically different engagement patterns, risk levels, and recovery likelihood.

📊

No Statistical Validation

Intervention decisions made without hypothesis testing, controlled experimentation, or significance analysis.

⚠️

No Churn Early Warning

872 students at high churn risk (>70%) with no predictive system to identify and prioritize proactive intervention.

System Design

End-to-End Analytics Architecture

An 8-layer analytics pipeline spanning event-level behavioral tracking, dimensional data modeling, statistical analysis, predictive modeling, and intervention intelligence.

1

Event-Level Data Generation

Simulated 592K behavioral events using statistical distributions — modeling app opens, absences, study sessions, quiz attempts, and professor decisions.

2

Data Warehouse

Structured storage with dimensional modeling — fact tables for events/sessions, dimension tables for users/professors, indexed for sub-second analytical querying.

3

ETL Pipeline

Extract-Transform-Load pipeline converting raw event logs into user profiles, session aggregates, daily KPI metrics, cohort assignments, and experiment groups.

4

Analytics Engine

Compute conversion funnels, cohort retention curves (D1/D7/D30), DAU/WAU stickiness ratios, and a weighted composite Product Health Score.

5

Predictive Analytics

K-Means behavioral segmentation (4 clusters), Random Forest churn prediction (AUC 0.96), and Isolation Forest anomaly detection across daily metrics.

6

Insight Generation

Automated extraction of data-driven findings ranked by business impact — behavioral correlations, operational bottlenecks, and engagement patterns.

7

Statistical Experimentation

Controlled A/B testing with Student's t-test, confidence interval analysis, and intervention impact measurement using behavioral cohort comparisons.

8

Interactive Analytics Dashboard

9-section analytical interface with segment-filtered KPIs, drill-down funnels, retention heatmaps, and scenario simulation capabilities.
